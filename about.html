Shruti Gautam

About:

Shruti is a senior at Rock Bridge High School in Columbia, Missouri. In her freshman year, Shruti started her district’s feminine hygiene initiative and was given a $20,000 fund from the school board to run it. She introduced a bill to the state legislature and is working on researching the effect of period poverty on Missourian communities. Shruti is also a finance intern on the Nicole Galloway for Governor campaign and a member of the city’s Youth Advisory Council. She is the Student Council Vice-President, Captain of the Speech and Debate Team, President of Interact, and President of Girls Who Code. Shruti has also been involved with scientific research since she was 15, focusing on both pathological and behavioral neurology. She is currently part of two such projects, studying both the relationship between alcoholism and insomnia and the effects of MDD and PTSD through brain imaging. In her free time, Shruti loves to watch movies and attend film festivals.

Why Copula?

Shruti understands the importance of a variety of interests; however, this is not a luxury everyone can afford. Connections are often hard to make, and many students are regionally disadvantaged. One’s zip-code should not be a determining factor of education.

Rebecca Monge

About:

Rebecca is a senior at Carmel High School in Carmel, New York. As part of her school’s science research program, she examines the epidemiological effects of climate change in New York State, predicting changes in Lyme disease case patterns and infection risk for future years. Besides conducting research, Rebecca also runs track and cross country for her school, and is a member of the varsity squad in both sports. Outside of school and research, Rebecca volunteers in a Language Exchange program where she helps teach English to Spanish speakers in her town, helping create a curriculum that not only helps them learn a new language, but also speak it in a professional context. In her free time, Rebecca enjoys finding new trails, reading scientific literature, and learning how to code, in addition to actively seeking new ways to better herself and her community.

Why Copula?

From her own experience with her mentor, Rebecca understands the importance of such a connection when conducting research, applying to internships, and gaining valuable hands-on experience. Through the Copula Program, she hopes to bring the same opportunity to countless other students like her.

Neil Mehta

About:

Neil is a senior at Ward Melville H.S. interested in public health. In his hometown, he runs Mission: Toothbrush, a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that has raised over $65,000 in essential hygiene supplies—dental supplies, infant care products, and menstrual hygiene products—for vulnerable populations on Long Island. Neil is an editor for his school newspaper, President of his school’s math team, and Drum Major for his district marching band. Outside of Ward Melville, Neil conducts biomedical research at Stony Brook University—in 2019, he studied the role of Interleukin-22 signaling in the gut inflammatory response. He is now studying clinical outcome prediction in COVID-19 patients. When not in the lab, Neil organizes district music events as President of his school’s Tri-M Music Honor Society, where he helps provide free instrumental lessons to young musicians. In his free time, Neil enjoys creative writing and playing Tetris.

Why Copula?

One of the most direct ways to improve the health of a community is to improve access to education. Through the Copula Program, Neil hopes to close regional and socioeconomic disparities in higher education and help students from all backgrounds live healthier, more informed lives.

Amal Yazeji

About:

Amal is a senior in the Academy for Medical Science Technology at the Bergen County Academies in Hackensack, New Jersey. Amal has conducted cell biology research since she was 15, for which she has received numerous grants. Her project focuses on elucidating the underpinnings of osteolytic disorder pathogenesis through modulation of reactive oxygen species. At her school, Amal is President of the National Honor Society and Vice-President of the Women’s Empowerment Club. Outside of research and school work, she volunteers with First Friends of NJ and NY, a non-profit organization that provides relief for detained persons through legislative advocacy, resettlement assistance, and detainee visitations. She is also a student in Columbia University’s Science Honors Program and a competitor of HOSA-Future Health Professionals. In her free time, she loves reading David Sedaris’ essay collections.

Why Copula?

Amal is passionate about advocacy for gender and racial equity. She hopes that through the Copula Program, valuable mentorship opportunities could become to those who might otherwise not have access to them.
